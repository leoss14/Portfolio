Research Question

  • Combined econometric analysis with machine learning approaches
  • Used Economic Complexity Index (ECI) as primary outcome measure

The Economic Complexity Index

What is ECI?

A measure developed by Harvard Growth Lab capturing the "know-how" accumulated by a country.

Tracks transition from low complexity activities (subsistence farming, oil extraction) to high complexity (electronics, machinery, chemicals).

Method: Based on trade data using Diversity (products exported) and Ubiquity (countries exporting them)

Why ECI?

  • ‚ñ∏ Better Coverage: Traditional value-added measures have poor data for developing countries
  • ‚ñ∏ Granularity: Product-level detail to identify specific strengths and weaknesses

Methodology

Econometric Model

$$ \begin{aligned} \log(ECI_{it}) = \beta_0 &+ \beta_1 \log(HCI_{it}) + \beta_2 \log(GFCF_{it}) + \beta_3 \log(Production_{it}) \\ &+ \beta_4 PoliticalStability_{it} + \beta_5 RuleOfLaw_{it} + \beta_6 Trade_{it} \\ &+ \beta_7 [\log(HCI_{it}) \times \log(Production_{it})] \\ &+ \beta_8 [\log(GFCF_{it}) \times \log(Production_{it})] \\ &+ \beta_9 \log(ECI_{i,t-1}) + \epsilon_{it} \end{aligned} $$
Variables
  • ECI: Economic Complexity Index
  • HCI: Human Capital Index
  • GFCF: Gross Fixed Capital Formation
  • Production: Mineral production value pc
Estimation
  • Method: OLS with country fixed effects
  • Errors: Clustered by country
  • Sample: Resource-rich countries

Key Findings

Convergent Results: Econometrics + Machine Learning

  • Human Capital √ó Production interaction is the strongest predictor - returns to education compound with resource extraction
  • Institutions matter: Rule of Law and Political Stability consistently significant across all models
  • Resource Curse rejected: When controlling for institutions and human capital, resources don't inherently harm development
  • Investment crucial: Gross Fixed Capital Formation shows strong positive effects
  • ML validates theory: Economic complexity framework predictions confirmed through predictive modeling
